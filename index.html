Economics @Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence for Real Life Economics

Research Project and Technological Solution developed by Decision Analysis And Statistics Group Universidad Politécnica de Madrid

Overview

The ECONOMICS @INTELLIGENCE initiative started at the Universidad Politécnica de Madrid (UPM) pursues a twofold goal: (i) the creation of an easy-to-access economic database, both for viewing and downloading data; and (ii) the online data exploitation using computational techniques in the field of Artificial Intelligence -our so called @I. The data that would be integrated initially include a considerable number of official variables that are already published in the National Institute of Statistics (INE) of Spain and the World Development Indicators of the World Bank.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Economics @Intelligence?

Economics @Intelligence is a research project developed at Universidad Politécnica de Madrid that provides access to economic data, analytical tools and research outputs to support learning and research activities.

What is Lighthouse?

Lighthouse is the main data platform of the project. It allows users to access, consult and explore economic data obtained from official and reliable sources.

What is Radar?

Radar is a tool designed to analyse and visualise economic and social phenomena through interactive dashboards, statistical methods and data-driven insights.

What is Spyglass?

Spyglass is a repository of articles and reflections created by students and researchers after using the project's tools and datasets to explore economic topics.

What are Dash-boats?

Dash-boats are student research projects and interactive dashboards developed under the supervision of the Economics @Intelligence team, showcasing practical applications of the available tools and data.

Who can benefit from this website?

The website is intended for students, researchers, educators and anyone interested in exploring economic data, learning from research outputs or developing analytical skills.

Researchers can use the platform to access structured economic data, explore analytical tools and discover research outputs that may support future studies and collaborations.

Students can use the platform to access datasets, develop academic projects, improve their data analysis skills and gain experience working with real-world economic information.
